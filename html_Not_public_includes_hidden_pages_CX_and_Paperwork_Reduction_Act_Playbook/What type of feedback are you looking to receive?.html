Thinking about the lifecycle of a project or product can be helpful in framing the potential Paperwork Reduction Act requests. For example, creating a research plan for discovery and evaluation that leverages feedback at a smaller scale can reduce the burden included information collection requests within the product.
The Customer Experience Center of Excellence recommends getting feedback at a smaller scale first to better inform any large scale feedback you plan on receiving in the future.

Discovery (or generative)

Feedback to define direction

Discovery (generative) feedback is a critical to define the direction of a project. Listening sessions are already being used within parts of CMS and as a customer experience professional you can join already in-process calls to listen in.  In addition, smaller observational sessions using low-fidelity prototypes can provide early feedback that saves resources as the product matures.

Feedback to define shape of product

Generative feedback can also be used to reduce the scope (burden) of a product. A quick field study or user interviews with a handful of participants can be a great way to improve a project earlier in the process while also being mindful of the Paperwork Reduction Act.

Evaluative

Feedback to define improvements

In some cases it is best practice to test with less than 9 people. The Nielsen Norman Group states that: Elaborate usability tests are a waste of resources. The best results come from testing no more than 5 users and running as many small tests as you can afford. (Why you only need to test with 5 users.)
If you’re conducting simple usability research consider having low sample sizes, in a frequent cadence, while ensuring that the part of the product that you’re receiving feedback on is not duplicative of previous research sessions.

Feedback to define opportunities

Conducting customer feedback survey’s can require an Information Collection request. In some cases providing an open ended survey question (such as: please tell us how we can improve ________) can provide opportunities to collect customer experience feedback. If your team needs a more robust question set to learn how customers interact with your process you should consider reaching out to OSORA to set up a more robust information collection package, while also maintaining your lower burden open ended survey.

For advice or consultation on your CX research plan,  please contact the Customer Experience Center of Excellence (CXCoE).
